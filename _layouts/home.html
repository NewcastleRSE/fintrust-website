PUBLICATION HIGHLIGHT

Simulating the Effects of Social Presence on Trust

In the past decade, the financial industry has experienced a technology revolution. While we witness a rapid progression towards a cashless society and financial services that rely on socio-and-emotionally intelligent bots.

We investigated features linked to bot trust, categorizing the top traits expected from a financial chatbot before consumers place trust in them. Primarily, data security mattered to a large extent in the context of financial services, whether or not socio-emotional features were present.

Accuracy of bot messages and its conversational style were important features, whereas bot name, how the bot sounds and whether it resembles a human in appearance appeared to be inconsequential. Our results imply that task-oriented financial bots are preferred.
